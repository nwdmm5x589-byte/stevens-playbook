HOA & Permit Checklist
Verify before you break ground on anything
Do these before any contractor conversation. Deed restrictions in Polk County lake communities can prohibit STR use, auxiliary structures, or commercial activity. Know your constraints first.
HOA / Deed Restriction Research — Do This First
Pull your deed from Polk County Appraisal District Go to polkcad.org → search 382 Stevens Ln → download deed. Look for restrictive covenants, especially re: short-term rentals and accessory dwelling units (ADUs).
Confirm whether Memorial Point UD has STR restrictions Your water provider is Memorial Point UD (it's on your bills). That's a municipal utility district — they may have deed restrictions attached to the subdivision. Call them directly.
Check for HOA — if one exists, get their CC&Rs Lake communities in Livingston often have informal HOAs. Even if you don't pay dues, deed restrictions may bind you. Ask neighbors or check with title company.
Verify STR is currently legal at your address Livingston city limits may have different rules than unincorporated Polk County. Confirm you're operating legally — most lake properties outside city limits are fine.
Check if ADU / guest house is permitted on your lot Polk County has setback requirements for accessory structures. Typically 5–10ft from property line minimum. Confirm your lot size allows the proposed footprint.
Permit Requirements — Polk County / City of Livingston
Building permit — required for any new structure over 200 sqft Contact Polk County Permits office. For unincorporated county land: (936) 327-6813. Expect 2–6 week approval timeline.
Electrical permit — required for new panel / hookup Must be pulled by licensed electrician. Your TGM connection with Trinity (TDLR apprentice) won't qualify for this — need a licensed electrician to pull the permit.
Plumbing permit — required for MIL suite bathroom/kitchen New septic capacity may be required if your current system is sized only for the main house. Get a septic inspection before finalizing plans.
Flood zone check — FEMA FIRM map Lake Livingston area has significant flood zone areas. Confirm your parcel's flood designation before building — it affects insurance, financing, and what you can build.
Septic capacity — confirm existing system supports ADU Adding a MIL suite adds load to your septic. Most systems in the area are sized for the main house only. May require expansion or second system.
STR Tax & Business Obligations — Texas
Hotel Occupancy Tax — state + local
Airbnb collects and remits Texas state HOT (6%) automatically. But Polk County and City of Livingston may have additional local HOT. Confirm with your CPA whether you owe any local HOT Airbnb doesn't collect.
Texas Sales Tax Permit — confirm STR is covered
You have your Texas Sales and Use Tax Permit for TGM. Confirm with your CPA whether STR income flows through TGM Group LLC or separate.
Airbnb 1099-K — keep for your CPA
Airbnb will issue a 1099-K if you exceed $600 in annual payments. Ensure this reconciles to your reported income.

Long-Term Guest Policy — When to Say No
The young adult situation (parents booked for 20-year-olds for 3 months, left house dirty) is a recoverable lesson. Here's how to prevent it going forward.
No monthly bookings May–October, period
Block monthly pricing in your calendar for those months. Non-negotiable.
For 30+ day stays, require a video call first
Anyone staying a month or more should speak with you. You'll know in 5 minutes if they're the right fit.
Require a damage deposit for all monthly stays
Airbnb's standard policies may not cover damage from a dirty house. A separate damage deposit agreement gives you recourse.
Midstay check-in at 30 days for multi-month
For a 2–3 month stay, do a quick walk-through or send your cleaner at the 30-day mark. Catch problems before they become disasters.
